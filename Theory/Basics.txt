Swaras:
7 basic swaras

Shadja
Rishabha
Gandhara
Madhyama
Panchama
Dhaivata
Nishada

Positions:
s1 Ṣhaḍja
r1 Shuddha Rishabha
r2 Chatushruti Rishabha / g1 Shuddha Gandhara
r3 Shatshruti Rishabha / g2 Sadharana Gandhara
g3 Antara Gandhara
m1 Shuddha Madhyama
m2 Prathi Madhyama
p  Panchama
d1 Shuddha Dhaivata
d2 Chatushruti Dhaivata / n1 Shuddha Nishada
d3 Shatshruti Dhaivata / n2 Kaishiki Nishada
n3 Kakali Nishada

s, r1, r2=g1, r3=g2, g3, m1, m2, p, d1, d2=n1, d3=n2, n3

=======
Octaves:
lower s to higher S is an octave

lower octave = Mandra Sthayi = chest voice
middle octave = Madhya Sthayi = throat voice
higher octave = Tara Syhayi = head voice
=======
Shruti = Pitch
There are 22 theoretical positions (22 'kattai's).
12 are practical.

Western notations for these 12 positions and their numberings.
1   C
1.5 C#
2   D
2.5 D#
3   E
4   F
4.5 F#
5   G
5.5 G#
6   A
6.5 A#
7   B

Male singers: Pitch will be from C to D. i.e, 1 -> 2 (C or C# generally)
Female singers: Pitch will be from F to A. i.e, 4 -> 6 (G or G# generally)
Instruments: D, D# or E generally

0 d1 n1
1 d1 n2
2 d1 n3
3 d2 n1

s, p and S are fixed

Number of possible melakartas are 6 * 2 * 6 = 72

Melakarta Number is first 2 syllable reversed.
Example: Mayamalavagowla
Ma = 5
Ya = 1
Melakarta number: 15

15 < 36 -> m1

15 - 1 = 14
14/6
quotient = 2 (r1 g3)
remainder = 2 (d1 n3)

s p are fixed

So, Mayamalavagowla is `s r1 g3 m1 p d1 n3 S`
